A little about me and where I've lived

A little about Lille, a little about Brussels, a little more about myself

Life in Lille

Lille, located in northern France, is a dynamic city with a rich blend of French and Flemish influences. Its stunning architecture, vibrant cultural scene, and historic sites like the Grand Place create a welcoming atmosphere. Known for its artistic heritage and university life, Lille offers a unique mix of tradition and modernity that attracts both tourists and students.


Life in Brussels


Just to the east, Brussels, Belgium's capital, is a vibrant city where history and modernity meet. Home to the European Union's institutions, it boasts diverse architecture, from medieval buildings to Art Nouveau gems. The city is also famous for its cuisine, offering delicious waffles, fries, and chocolate, reflecting Belgium’s rich culinary traditions.

Languages

As an Irish person, I started learning Irish at four, and it’s always felt like a core part of who I am and who I have become. Later, I took up Spanish and Dutch — Spanish for its global reach, and Dutch for its unique insight into Belgian culture. Speaking all three has broadened my worldview and deepened my connection to different people and places.


Sports


Playing Gaelic football, hurling, and golf keeps me active and connected to different sides of myself. Football and hurling root me in Irish tradition, building teamwork, speed, and resilience. Golf provides calm focus and a personal challenge. Each sport brings something unique, and together they help me stay fit, sharp, and deeply connected to culture and discipline.
