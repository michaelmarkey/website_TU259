A little about me and where I've lived

A little about Lille, a little about Brussels, a little more about myself

Life on the Continent

After finishing my degree at Trinity College Dublin, I moved to Lille with the idea of spending just a year or two as an English teaching assistant. What began as a short-term plan quickly turned into something more permanent, as I found myself drawn to the city’s pace, atmosphere, and charm.


Lille offered a striking blend of old and new—its red brick architecture and cobbled streets giving way to lively squares and a thriving student scene. The city felt both historic and forward-looking, and that balance suited me well as I transitioned into graduate studies and then doctoral research at the university.


Over time, I built a strong social network made up of French colleagues, international researchers, and fellow expatriates. My weekends were often spent cycling through the northern countryside or hopping on a train to nearby Belgium, making the most of Lille’s location at the heart of Europe.

Route from Lille to Brussels


Years later, I relocated to Brussels to take on freelance work in language consulting. The shift brought a new rhythm—faster-paced and more international, with the influence of the EU institutions shaping the city’s energy. Brussels offered new cultural layers, from the bilingual street signs to its famously eclectic neighborhoods.


Throughout my two decades abroad, I stayed closely connected to Ireland through regular visits home. But France and Belgium became more than places I lived—they shaped how I think, work, and experience the world. What started as a post-college adventure became the most formative chapter of my life.

A little bit about me

I grew up in Dublin in the ’80s and ’90s playing Gaelic football and hurling with Ballinteer St John’s. My father, a founding member of Stackstown Golf Club, taught me to play golf, and I spent many mornings on the fairway working on my drives.

The fastest game on grass!


I started learning Irish at four, studied both French and German and spent summers in the Gaeltacht improving my Irish. More recently I started learning Spanish and, while living in Brussels, I took Dutch classes.


After college I moved to Lille in France. Not many people in Ireland know about it but it’s the biggest French city north of Paris. It’s also the most Flemish, with a wonderful mix of Belgian and local traditions and architecture. When I lived there, I used to cycle from Lille to Kortrijk along the Flemish country roads.


One of the most interesting aspects of the culture there is the carnivals, like Dunkirk Carnival and the Carnival of Binche. Also, if you ever get the chance, go to the Gentse Feesten…


Beyond travel and sport, I’ve always been drawn to nightlife. I recently joined the TU Dublin DJ society out of pure curiosity and am getting back into club culture in Dublin. I’m still learning beat-matching but hopefully some day you’ll come across one of my mixes.


My favourite pastime is to explore on foot. In Dublin, it’s up and down the coast. In Lille, it’s around the Citadelle of Lille. In Brussels, it’s the Coulée Verte, a huge loop around the region that takes you through the industrial past right up to the modern metropolis.
